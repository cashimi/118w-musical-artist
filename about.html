ABOUT MALCOLM TODD

Malcolm Todd Hobert, known professionally as Malcolm Todd, is an American singer-songwriter, musician, and record producer born on September 15, 2003, in Los Angeles, California. He is recognized for his blend of genres, including indie pop, alternative R&B, R&B, and bedroom pop.

Growing up surrounded by music, he developed an early passion for songwriting and performance, shaping a style that blends pop, R&B, and alternative influences with a modern, youthful energy.

Todd began his career during the COVID-19 pandemic, writing and producing his first extended play, Demos Before Prom, which contained songs from his high school years. While initially gaining attention by claiming his songs were unreleased tracks by Steve Lacy, his commercial breakthrough came in 2023.

His singles "Art House" and "Roommates" went viral on TikTok, leading to his signing with Columbia Records.

Malcolm Todd has also collaborated with emerging artists, blending his signature indie-pop style with experimental sounds. These collaborations have helped him expand his creative reach and explore new musical directions.

Beyond music, he is involved in producing content for social media and connecting with fans directly, creating a highly interactive and personal experience with his audience.
