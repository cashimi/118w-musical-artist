Biography

Malcolm Todd Hobert, known professionally as Malcolm Todd, is an American singer-songwriter, musician, and record producer born on September 15, 2003, in Los Angeles, California. He is recognized for his blend of genres, including indie pop, alternative R&B, R&B, and bedroom pop.

Todd began his career during the COVID-19 pandemic, writing and producing his first extended play, Demos Before Prom, which contained songs from his high school years. While initially gaining attention by claiming his songs were unreleased tracks by Steve Lacy, his commercial breakthrough came in 2023. His singles "Art House" and "Roommates" went viral on the video-sharing platform TikTok, leading to his signing with Columbia Records.

His early projects include the EP Shower Shoes in 2023 and the mixtape Sweet Boy, released in 2024. Todd followed this success with his self-titled debut studio album, Malcolm Todd, released on April 4, 2025. The album achieved commercial success, charting at number 49 on the Billboard 200. The lead single from the album, "Chest Pain (I Love)," marked his first appearance on the Billboard Hot 100, where it peaked at number 68. He was educated in music production at the University of Oregon.
